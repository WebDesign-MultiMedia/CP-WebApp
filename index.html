What is OEM Parts ?

Definition

These are parts made by the vehicle’s original manufacturer or an approved supplier. They are identical to the parts that originally came with your vehicle.

Quality and Fit

High quality, guaranteed fit, and reliability as they meet the exact specifications set by the carmaker.

Warranty

Often come with a warranty backed by the vehicle manufacturer.

Cost

Usually more expensive than aftermarket parts due to the branding and assurance of quality.

Availabilty

Primarily available at dealerships and authorized parts retailers.

Best for

Those who prefer reliability, want to maintain their vehicle’s factory condition, or are concerned about warranty coverage.

AfterMarket

Info

What is AfterMarket Parts ?

Definition

Parts made by third-party companies not associated with the vehicle’s original manufacturer. They serve as alternatives to OEM parts and are often designed to fit multiple models.

Quality and Fit

Quality can vary widely, from budget options to high-quality alternatives. Fit and finish may not always be as precise as OEM.

Warranty

Some come with warranties, but they vary by brand and part.

Cost

Typically less expensive than OEM parts, with a range of pricing depending on the brand and quality.

Availabilty

Widely available from auto parts stores, online retailers, and repair shops.

Best for

Budget-conscious buyers, those looking for more options, or those who don’t need the exact OEM specifications.
